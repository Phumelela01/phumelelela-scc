Our Programmes

Phumelela Special Care Centre offers a range of programmes designed to support the development and well-being of children and youth with disabilities. Our programmes include:

  • Specialised education and learning support
  • Daily meals (breakfast and lunch)
  • Life skills training and developmental activities
  • Skills development initiatives including gardening, baking, farming (pigs and chickens), beadwork, jam and juice making
  • Advocacy for disability inclusion and awareness

Skills Development Initiatives

We focus on practical skills that empower our learners to become independent and self-sufficient. These initiatives include:

  • Gardening: Teaching sustainable practices and providing fresh produce.
  • Baking: Developing culinary skills and creating products for sale.
  • Farming: Raising pigs and chickens to understand animal husbandry.
  • Beadwork: Encouraging creativity while producing beautiful crafts.
  • Jam and Juice Making: Learning food preservation techniques.
